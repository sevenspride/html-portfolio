A short history

Early life

My family came to the United states from Poland to start a better life because they wanted more for there kids than they had growing up. I was born in Philadelphia, PA and have two brothers that were born there as well. Growing up in Philadelphia wasnt the easiest thing in the world, the winters were cold and the streets were harsh, and in the neighborhood we grew up in there wasnt much room for growth, but life has a funny way of giving you chances when you least expect them.

When I got to middle school my friend Matt at the time introduced me to music for the first time, musical instruments that is. This is where I fell in love with playing he clarinet and learning everything there was to learn about Jazz, Orechestral arrangments ,music composition and so much more. I stuided for the rest of my middle school and high school career and managed to make some pretty substantial accomplishments such as.

  • Studying at Luzerne music school
  • Studying with Sam Caviezel from the Philadelphia Orchestra
  • Making first chair clarinet in Philadelphia Orchestra and concert band
  • Playing first clarinet in District Orchestra and concert band
  • Playing first clarinet in Regional Orchestra and concert band

After Highschool I chose not to go to college because I was afraid of taking on a lot of debt and not getting a job that would pay the debt. The field of study I was looking to get into was music education but the school I was going to go to only opted to have a performance art degree which I ultimitally decided not to pursue.

Adulthood

After my music I was lost and didn't really know what to do with myself. For 15 years I thought I was going to be an orchestral musician or at least a teacher but when that didnt pan out I started working the odd jobs here and there. The First job after highschool that I worked was AT&T where I would sell cell phones for 3 years and make a lot of close friendships with customers. However as time went on I realized that I wanted more for myself, I didnt just wanna sit here for my 20s selling phones and not growing my skills so I decided to take a gamble on myself and apply for new jobs, not really knowing what to expect.


After the job at AT&T I took a job with a company called Unifirst, everything on paper looked great but then I realized what it meant to be paid a salary. I thought the sound of 45000 a year for someone who was only 21 with no college education sounded great, that was until I started working 14 hour days. When I did the math I realized that I was being had and I had to get out of that situation since it wasnt sustainable for the long term.


Eventually I found a way out of Unifirst into a well paying Union job at a dairy plant called HP.Hood. here I stayed from 2016-2019 working as a machine operator who would do things such as filling machines with product, taking apart pistons and rebuilding filler heads, rebuilding and replacing O rings, washing the inside and outside of the machine with acids and caustics to get it ready for safe production use, and testing titrate samples. This was a good job for a long time even tho the hours were long but I soon found that I was getting too comfortable and staying stagnant . I had that same gnawing feeling that I had at AT&T , I didnt wanna get stale and I wanted more for myself again. At the same time I had a horribe sense of wonderlust too since all I've ever really seen was PA and the surrounding area. It wasa time for a change.


A change

The long days and same routines were starting to take a toll on me with my life. Everytime I drove home I'd always imagine what it would be like to, instead of going home, just keep driving west. My mind would wander and think about what it would be like to see towering red woods, snow capped mountains, barren deserts, sprawling cities, and everything else that the country had to offer. So I decided it was time for a change, it was time to get my CDL-A.
